Courses

Programs and courses offered by the Department of Information Technology

Diploma in Computer Teacher Training (CTTC)

This one-year program covers the fundamental concepts of computers and programming along with effective methodologies for teaching IT-based curricula. The course also includes English practice sessions to help students develop confidence, communication skills, and an engaging professional personality.

Diploma in Computer Applications (DCA - Regular)

This one-year program helps students explore the core areas of computer applications by learning the essentials and practical aspects of Computers, Operating Systems, and System Engineering. The course includes intensive lab sessions to ensure strong understanding of programming concepts and real-world software usage.

Diploma in Computer Applications (DCA - Fast Track)

This six-month fast-track program is designed to introduce students to core computer science concepts and logic-building techniques used in software application development. The course also provides in-depth training in office suite applications, preparing students for office administration and IT-based business environments.

Professional Diploma in Computerized Financial Accounting (PDCFA)

This six-month program begins by strengthening students’ data entry skills through structured keyboard typing practice using quick-learning software. The course further equips learners with operational-level skills in word processing, spreadsheets, and presentation tools of the Microsoft Office Suite, preparing them for office administration roles.

Professional Diploma in Desktop Publishing (PDDTP)

This six-month program offers hands-on training in desktop publishing software such as PageMaker, CorelDRAW, Photoshop, InDesign, and ISM. Students learn to create professional document layouts, typographic quality text, image editing, and visual design for both print and digital media.

Diploma in Office Automation (DOA)

This six-month program covers essential digital technologies used in modern automated office environments. The course trains students to manage daily administrative and office management tasks using digital tools applicable to private and public sector organizations, business firms, and NGOs.

Professional Diploma in Web Designing (PDWD)

This six-month program focuses on graphic designing using Photoshop and introduces students to web designing and functional development. The course trains learners to design attractive, user-centric websites and develop functional web applications, preparing them for the digital business environment where websites serve as the primary storefront for brands and organizations.

Professional Diploma in Computer Aided Design (PDCAD)

This six-month program is designed for architects, engineers, drafters, artists, and designers to create, modify, analyze, and optimize precise technical drawings and illustrations. The course provides hands-on training in AutoCAD (2D & 3D) along with Photoshop, enabling students to produce professional-quality engineering and design drawings efficiently.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about courses, admissions, and facilities offered by the Department of Information Technology

1. What courses are offered by the IT Department?

The IT Department offers diploma and certificate courses in Computer Applications, Programming, Web Designing, Office Automation, DTP, AutoCAD, and Teacher Training programs, designed to meet industry requirements.

2. What is the eligibility for IT courses?

Most courses require 10th standard pass or above. Certain advanced courses may require Plus Two.

3. Are the courses practical-oriented?

Yes. All courses focus on hands-on practical training, lab sessions, real-time projects, and skill development to prepare students for job roles in the IT industry.

4. What career opportunities are available after course completion?

Students can pursue careers such as Computer Operator, IT Support Specialist, Web Designer, Office Assistant, Programmer, DTP Operator, Teacher, and more, depending on the course chosen.

5. Does the department provide placement or career guidance support?

Yes. The department provides career guidance, skill enhancement sessions, and support for job readiness, helping students identify suitable career paths.
